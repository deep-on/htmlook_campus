## Week 5 — Synapses

> Cited from `lecture.md` slide 5. Midterm in 9 days.

**Three-bullet summary**:

- A synapse is the junction where one neuron signals the next via neurotransmitter (slide 2).
- The transmission goes AP → Ca²⁺ in → vesicle release → receptor binding → post-synaptic depolarisation (slide 3).
- Inputs that arrive within ~5 ms summate temporally; inputs across multiple sites summate spatially (slide 4).

**Worked example — temporal summation**:

Two excitatory inputs arrive 3 ms apart. Each EPSP alone is +8 mV at the
post-synaptic membrane (sub-threshold). The two EPSPs sum to roughly
+15 mV, pushing the post-synaptic neuron past the −55 mV threshold and
firing an action potential. (Cite: slide 4.)
