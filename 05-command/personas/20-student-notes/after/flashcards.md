---

**Q1.** What is a synapse?
**A1.** The junction where one neuron passes a signal to the next via neurotransmitter released into the cleft, binding receptors on the post-synaptic membrane.

---

**Q2.** Name the five steps of synaptic transmission, in order.
**A2.**
1. Action potential reaches pre-synaptic terminal.
2. Voltage-gated Ca²⁺ channels open.
3. Vesicles fuse and release neurotransmitter.
4. Neurotransmitter binds post-synaptic receptors; channels open.
5. Post-synaptic neuron depolarises (or hyperpolarises, if inhibitory).

---

**Q3.** What is the difference between temporal and spatial summation?
**A3.** Temporal summation = multiple EPSPs from the *same* input arriving close in time (~5 ms) sum together. Spatial summation = EPSPs from *different* inputs at multiple sites sum together.

---

**Q4.** Two EPSPs of +8 mV each arrive 3 ms apart at a post-synaptic neuron with threshold −55 mV (resting −70 mV). Will it fire?
**A4.** Yes — temporal summation gives roughly +15 mV, which pushes the membrane to about −55 mV, reaching threshold.
