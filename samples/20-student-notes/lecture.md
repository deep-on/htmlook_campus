## Slide 2. The synapse — overview

A synapse is the junction where one neuron passes a signal to the next.
The pre-synaptic terminal releases neurotransmitter into the cleft;
receptors on the post-synaptic membrane open ion channels.

## Slide 3. Sequence

1. Action potential reaches the pre-synaptic terminal.
2. Voltage-gated Ca²⁺ channels open; Ca²⁺ flows in.
3. Vesicles fuse with the membrane and release neurotransmitter.
4. Neurotransmitter binds post-synaptic receptors; channels open.
5. The post-synaptic neuron begins its own depolarisation (or hyperpolarisation, for inhibitory synapses).

## Slide 4. Worked example — EPSP summation

If two excitatory inputs arrive within 5 ms of each other, their EPSPs
sum (temporal summation) and may push the post-synaptic neuron past
threshold. Spatial summation works analogously across multiple inputs.

## Slide 5. Why this matters for the midterm

The midterm asks for the *sequence* and one *worked example*. Memorise
the five-step sequence and one summation case.
