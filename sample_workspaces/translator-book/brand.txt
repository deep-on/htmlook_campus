Title shown:    The Workshop · Chapter 3
Source book:    "Hammers, Nails, and Other Lies" by E. R. Halloran (English original)
Target:         Korean translation, footnoted scholarly edition
One-liner:      A printed page, two languages.

Palette
  --cream        #f4ecd8   warm paper background
  --ink          #1a1208   deep ink for body text
  --burgundy     #8b1a1a   accent (chapter number, drop caps, footnote rules)
  --rule         rgba(26, 18, 8, 0.18)   page rules and column dividers
  --footnote     #5a4838   muted brown for footnote body
  --fold         rgba(26, 18, 8, 0.06)   page-fold shadow

Typography
  Display       serif (Cormorant / Playfair / EB Garamond), 700 weight
                for chapter title, 400 italic for chapter epigraph
  Body          serif (EB Garamond / Source Serif), 400 weight,
                12pt-equivalent, justified columns, hyphenation off
  Korean body   Noto Serif KR / Pretendard Serif, 400 weight,
                matched x-height to the English column
  Folio         small caps, 1px tracking, very small
  Footnote      same family as body, 9pt-equivalent, hung indent

Motion language
  No bouncy easing. The page reveals like ink soaking in: the
  chapter header settles, then the English column flows in line
  by line, then the Korean column flows in matched paragraph by
  matched paragraph. Footnote markers pop only when the line they
  belong to has finished. The outro is a single page-flip — the
  whole composition rotates on the spine, revealing a hint of the
  next chapter's first line, then settles. No glitter, no glow,
  no kinetic typography.
