Chapter 3
The Workshop
English · source

The workshop did not look like the place a craftsman would choose. It was square, low-ceilinged, and lit by a single window that faced the wrong way for morning work.

A long bench ran the length of the room. On it lay tools that had been sharpened so often the steel was thinner than the handles, and a row of nails ordered by size, smallest to largest, the way a child might sort buttons.

He had inherited the bench from his father, who had inherited it from his, and the maker's mark¹ in the corner was older than the building itself.

A long bench ran the length of the room. On it lay tools that had been sharpened so often the steel was thinner than the handles, and a row of nails ordered by size, smallest to largest, the way a child might sort buttons.

He had inherited the bench from his father, who had inherited it from his, and the maker's mark¹ in the corner was older than the building itself.

한국어 · 번역

그 작업장은 장인이라면 고를 만한 곳이 아니었다. 네모반듯하고 천장이 낮았으며, 아침 작업에는 방향이 맞지 않는 창 하나로만 빛이 들었다.

긴 작업대가 방의 길이만큼 뻗어 있었다. 그 위에는 너무 자주 갈아 칼몸이 손잡이보다 얇아진 연장들과, 아이가 단추를 정렬하듯 작은 것에서 큰 것 순으로 늘어놓은 못들이 놓여 있었다.

그는 작업대를 아버지에게서 물려받았고, 아버지는 또 그 아버지에게서 물려받았다. 모서리에 새겨진 장인의 인장¹은 건물 자체보다 오래된 것이었다.

¹ Maker's mark — a metalsmith's signature stamped into the work.
¹ 장인의 인장 — 장인이 자신의 작품에 새겨 넣는 고유한 표식.
