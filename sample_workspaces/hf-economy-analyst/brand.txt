Macro Brief 2026 — fictional weekly macro newsletter for the
economy-analyst scenario. Portrait single-page reports for mobile
readers (Substack-style swipeable, Threads / Instagram inline).

Tagline:        Mobile-first macro briefs.
URL:            macrobrief.report
One-liner:      Hook number. One chart. Three take-aways. Cited.

Format
  Frame         9:16 portrait, 1080×1920
  Sections      hook number → small chart → three bullets → footer
  Footer        "Macro Brief · 2026 W17" wordmark + URL

Palette
  --navy        #0c1e3d   deep navy background, Bloomberg-terminal calm
  --navy-2      #112c5a   surface elevation
  --gold        #fbbf24   primary accent (number, chart bar tip)
  --gold-2      #f59e0b   accent shadow / hover
  --cream       #f5e6c8   soft cream body text
  --muted       rgba(245, 230, 200, 0.55)   secondary text on navy
  --bar-track   rgba(245, 230, 200, 0.12)   chart bar track
  --green       #4ade80   positive prints
  --red         #f87171   negative prints

Typography
  Display       Inter / Pretendard, 800 weight, very tight tracking,
                used for the hook number ("+0.4 ppt"). Motion-blur
                kinetic stagger by character on entry.
  Body          Inter / Pretendard, 500-600 weight. Three-bullet
                take-aways reveal one-by-one with a 0.25s stagger.
  Mono (URL)    SF Mono / Menlo / ui-monospace, 4px tracking,
                used for the footer wordmark "MB · 2026 W17".

Motion language
  Entry         Hook number characters stagger in with 12px y-shift
                + 8px blur + back.out(1.4). Bar chart fills
                left-to-right, gold tip lights last (sine.inOut).
                Bullets reveal one-by-one with a leading "▸" gold
                marker. Footer wordmark dissolves in last.
  Pacing        Calm. No bounce. Suit + Bloomberg vibe — confident,
                quiet, never garish.

Copy guardrails
  Hook number   Always a single delta with units (e.g. "+0.4 ppt",
                "−1.2 %", "$3.2 T"). Sign visible.
  Three bullets Each ≤ 9 words. Verb-led. No marketing fluff. No
                exclamation marks. End with a citable source noun
                ("BLS", "Fed minutes", "ECB SDW").
  Source line   Always under bullets, muted cream, prefixed with
                "Source · ". One link only.
  No emoji. No exclamation marks. No "stunning" / "amazing" /
  "game-changing" — analyst voice, not influencer voice.

Distinct from the four other personas
  Where hf-claude / hf-llama lean amber+coral kinetic typography
  and Vault Local AI's padlock outro, Macro Brief is navy+gold
  portrait — a mobile financial card, not a launch teaser. The
  bar chart fill is the visual hook, the hook number is the
  emotional hook.
