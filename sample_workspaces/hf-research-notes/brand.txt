Apparatus Notes — fictional researcher's notebook for the research-notes scenario.

Domain:         Machine-learning research (vision-language models)
Tagline:        Read the paper. Mark the finding. Cite the source.
URL:            apparatus.notes
Paper shown:    Chen et al. 2026 — "Sparse Cross-Modal Attention for
                Long-Context Vision-Language Models" (NeurIPS-style)
Section dive:   Methods §3.2 + Results §4.1
One-liner:      Three findings, three cites, one bibliography append.

Palette
  --paper       #faf6ef   warm parchment background
  --ink         #1e293b   deep slate body text
  --ink-soft    #475569   secondary slate
  --rule        #e7e0d2   thin parchment rule
  --cite        #7e22ce   citation purple (marker, [1][2][3], DOI label)
  --link        #0891b2   link cyan (DOI URL, hyperlink)
  --highlight   rgba(126, 34, 206, 0.10)   citation highlight wash
  --accent-ink  #0f172a   strong slate for the wordmark

Typography
  Display       "Lora", "Source Serif Pro", Georgia, serif — 700 weight
                for paper title, 600 for section heads. Tight tracking on
                large display, normal on body.
  Body          "Charter", "Source Serif Pro", Georgia, serif — 400 with
                1.65 line-height. Reads like a published page.
  Mono (DOI,
   citekey)     "JetBrains Mono", "SF Mono", ui-monospace — used only
                for DOI strings, citekeys (e.g. chen2026sparse), and
                line-number gutters in the notes.

Motion language
  Page-turn metaphor — the paper title fades up first, then the three
  key-finding rows rise one by one with their citation markers
  ([1] [2] [3]) attaching last. Bibliography slides in from below
  like a footnote drawer opening. Wordmark dissolves in over the
  warm parchment at the end. No sparkles, no glow — this is a
  scholarly notebook, not a launch trailer.

Copy guardrails
  Three findings:    each is one sentence, present tense, technical
                     diction (e.g. "Sparse cross-modal attention cuts
                     FLOPs by 38% on >32k-token contexts.").
  Citation markers:  [1] [2] [3] — bracketed numerals in citation
                     purple, attached to the end of each finding.
  Bibliography:      author-year-title-DOI, author family name first,
                     DOI rendered as a link in citation purple.
  Wordmark:          "Apparatus Notes" — serif display, deep slate,
                     small "the researcher's notebook" tagline below.
  Voice:             dry, scholarly, factual. No exclamation marks,
                     no emoji, no marketing fluff.
  Avoid:             "groundbreaking", "revolutionary", "the first to",
                     "AI-powered" — this is for a working researcher,
                     not a press release.
