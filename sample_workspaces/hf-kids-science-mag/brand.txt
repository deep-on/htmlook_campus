Wonder Lab Kids — fictional monthly children's science magazine for the
kids-science-mag scenario. Single-page portrait magazine cards for
swipeable mobile reading (Substack Kids, Kakao Page, school newsletters).

Tagline:        Adult science → kids' page, in one workflow.
URL:            wonderlabkids.com
One-liner:      Cite a paragraph. Get a kids' page. Bubble + 3-step + glossary.

Format
  Frame         9:16 portrait, 1080×1920
  Issue         Monthly · Issue #15 (cover question: "왜 비누 거품은 둥글까?")
  Sections      masthead → curiosity bubble → 3-step experiment → answer + glossary
  Footer        "Wonder Lab Kids · Issue #15" wordmark

Palette
  --cobalt      #2563eb   masthead bar / step pill outlines (smart, not childish)
  --cobalt-2    #1d4ed8   masthead deep edge
  --yellow      #fbbf24   curiosity bubble fill (warm, inviting)
  --yellow-2    #f59e0b   bubble shadow / hover
  --orange      #ea580c   step accent / step-num pill (poppy, attention-grab)
  --orange-2    #c2410c   step pill deep edge
  --cream       #fef3c7   body card surface (paper-like, gentle)
  --ink         #0f172a   primary body text (high contrast on cream)
  --muted       rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.55)   secondary body text
  --line        rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.12)   hairline rules

Typography
  Display       Inter / Pretendard, 800 weight, tight tracking, used for
                the cover question ("왜 비누 거품은 둥글까?"). Kinetic
                stagger by character on entry — no bounce, no tilt.
  Body          Inter / Pretendard, 500-600 weight. Bubble + steps reveal
                with 0.25 s stagger.  Body copy stays 500 weight on
                cream.
  Mono          SF Mono / Menlo, 4 px tracking, used only for the issue
                badge "ISSUE #15" and the wordmark footer.

Motion language
  Entry         Masthead drops in from top (-20 px y).  Cover question
                staggers in by character with 8 px y-shift, no blur.
                Yellow curiosity bubble pops with back.out(1.4).
                Step pills "1 → 2 → 3" land left-to-right with 0.25 s
                stagger.  Glossary chip slides in last.
  Pacing        Friendly, not frantic.  Calm enough that a 9-year-old
                reading at home keeps up; bright enough to feel like a
                magazine, not a textbook.

Copy guardrails
  Cover Q       One question, in Korean.  No exclamation marks, no
                cutesy emoji.
  Curiosity     One sentence, ≤ 18 words. Hook the reader's curiosity
                without giving the answer away.
  3-step copy   Each step ≤ 6 Korean words. Verb-led ("물에 비누 넣기",
                "빨대로 불기", "관찰하기"). Step numbers in the orange
                pill.
  Vocabulary    1-3 glossary chips. Each: term + one-line plain-Korean
                definition. Term in cobalt, definition in ink.
  Source        Always under the answer, muted ink, prefixed with "출처 ·".
                Cite the adult-level paper or textbook in one line.
  No emoji. No exclamation marks. No "재미있어요!" / "신기해요!" — kids
  voice should be curious + confident, not condescending.

Distinct from the other personas
  Where economy-analyst is navy+gold portrait financial cards and
  press-editor / dev-pr-docs run amber+green landscape, Wonder Lab
  Kids is cobalt+yellow+orange on a cream paper card — it must feel
  like a printed magazine page, not an app screen.  The curiosity
  bubble is the visual hook, the cover question is the emotional
  hook.
