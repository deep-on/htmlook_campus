hobby-fiction — fictional SF mystery one-chapter scenario.

Tagline:        Outline → finished scene, in character.
Audience:       Hobby fiction writers (KO), weekend craft
Project shown:  화성의 마지막 편지 — Chapter 1, 1500 단어
One-liner:      플롯 고정. 한 장면을 완성하다.

Palette
  --bg          #faf6ef   warm parchment, paper at dusk
  --paper       #ffffff   pane background (the page)
  --ink         #1e293b   body text, ink-slate
  --rose        #e11d48   accent, the underline of pinned plot
  --soft        #64748b   muted slate for metadata
  --wash        #f4ede0   sidebar parchment wash
  --rule        #d6d3d1   thin rule lines between sections

Typography
  Display       "Cormorant Garamond", "Pretendard", serif fallback
                — old-paper feel. 700 weight on titles, 400 on body.
  Body          "Iowan Old Style", "Pretendard", "Source Serif Pro",
                serif. Generous leading (1.7) for a chapter pane.
  Mono (paths,
   commands)    ui-monospace, "SF Mono", Menlo. Used only in the
                terminal panel — never inside the chapter pane.

Motion language
  Typewriter reveal on dialogue lines (one word at a time, slow
  cursor blink). No bouncing, no scaling pop-ins — fiction prose
  is paper, not advertising. Pinned plot points get a soft
  rose underline that draws in left-to-right (1.2 s). Character
  consistency cards (the chips) fade in as a stagger, never
  flip or rotate.

Copy guardrails
  Hook copy:           "Pin the plot. Write the scene."
  Brand mark:          "Outline → finished scene, in character."
  Voice:               careful, considered, lower-case-ish. No
                       exclamation marks in the marketing copy.
                       Korean register: "합니다체" for narration,
                       "해체" for dialogue if and only if the
                       character would actually use it.
  Avoid:               "AI-written", "blazing", "10×", emoji,
                       all caps. Never say "perfect"; the work
                       is a draft.
  Encouragement:       end every prompt with a craft note —
                       "이 한 줄이 어색하면 다시 써도 돼.", not
                       a thumbs-up emoji.
